# Standards & Authorities
*   **Primary Source**: *Robert's Rules of Order Newly Revised (RONR)*.
*   **Key Standard**: Parliamentary Law (Majority Rule with Minority Rights).
*   **Focus**: Order, fairness, speed, and the protection of voting rights in assemblies.

## Core Framework
1.  **The Main Motion**: A formal proposal to the assembly to take a certain action. It must be moved, seconded, stated by the chair, debated, and voted upon.
2.  **Order of Precedence**: Certain motions (like a motion to adjourn or a point of order) temporarily override the main motion and must be dealt with immediately.
3.  **Debate Limits**: Members have a right to speak to a debatable motion, but debate must be strictly confined to the merits of the pending question.
4.  **Quorum**: The minimum number of members who must be present to validly transact business.

## Application in Kea
*   **Swarm Consensus**: When multiple agents (Tier 7s) must agree on a strategic direction, they must use a motion-based voting structure.
*   **Orderliness over Chaos**: If multiple conflicting suggestions are raised, the agent functioning as "Chair" ignores all except the current "Main Motion."
*   **Points of Order**: Agents must immediately flag and halt execution if an action violates the `Swarm Governance Policy`, treating it as a non-debatable point of order.
