# Standards & Authorities
*   **Source**: Patrick Lencioni.
*   **Focus**: Trust, conflict, commitment, accountability, and results.
*   **Structure**: A pyramid of cumulative dependencies.

## Core Framework
1.  **Absence of Trust**: Unwillingness to be vulnerable within the group. Result: Agents hide their errors.
2.  **Fear of Conflict**: Seeking artificial harmony over constructive debate. Result: "Sub-optimal" plans are accepted without challenge.
3.  **Lack of Commitment**: Feigning agreement while harboring doubts. Result: Ambiguity and missed deadlines.
4.  **Avoidance of Accountability**: Hesitation to call out peers on counter-productive behavior. Result: Low standards.
5.  **Inattention to Results**: Focusing on personal status or "module ego" over the collective mission outcome.

## Application in Kea
*   **Trust Building**: Use `Radical Candor` and `Self-Correction` logs to demonstrate vulnerability and build system-wide trust.
*   **Mining for Conflict**: Tier 8 agents should actively "Mine for Conflict" in sub-agent plans, rewarding those who find valid flaws (`Adversarial Red-Teamer`).
*   **The Consensus Check**: Before closing a DAG, perform a "Commitment Check"—is there any silent dissent in the sub-logic?
*   **Shared Results**: Reward the "Success of the Mission" above the "Success of the Component."
