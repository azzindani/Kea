# Standards & Authorities
*   **Primary Source**: The Four Geneva Conventions (1949) and their Additional Protocols.
*   **Key Standard**: Distinction between combatants and civilians; humane treatment of non-combatants.
*   **Focus**: Limiting the barbarity of war through universal rules of conduct.

## Core Framework
1.  **Distinction**: At all times, a distinction must be made between the civilian population and combatants. Attacks must only be directed against military objectives.
2.  **Proportionality**: Launching an attack which may be expected to cause incidental loss of civilian life or injury to civilians which would be excessive in relation to the concrete and direct military advantage anticipated is prohibited.
3.  **Humane Treatment**: Persons taking no active part in the hostilities shall in all circumstances be treated humanely, without any adverse distinction founded on race, color, religion, gender, or wealth.
4.  **Protection of the Wounded and Sick**: Members of the armed forces who are wounded or sick shall be respected and protected in all circumstances.
