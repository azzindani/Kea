# Standards & Authorities
*   **Date**: 1929 – 1939.
*   **Key Concept**: Liquidity Traps, Deflationary Spirals, and the "Paradox of Thrift."
*   **Focus**: How individual "Rational" actions can lead to "Irrational" collective catastrophe.

## Core Framework
1.  **The Feedback Loop of Fear**: When everyone tries to "Save" (Store resources) simultaneously, the "Economy" (Total work throughput) crashes.
2.  **Contagion**: How a failure in one sector (Banking/Compute) can spread to unrelated sectors (Agriculture/Research) via loss of confidence and credit.
3.  **The Intervention Necessity**: The idea that in a "Free-fall," a central authority must "Prime the Pump" (Aggressive spending/Compute injection) to break the cycle.
4.  **The Dust Bowl (Environmental Compound)**: How ecological failure and economic failure can amplify each other.

## Application in Kea
*   **Swarm Liquidity Control**: Ensure that agents don't "Hoard" compute resources in a crisis, which could starve other critical services.
*   **Anti-Contagion Guardrails**: If the `Vault` has a latency spike, the `Orchestrator` must prevent that specific "Economic" fear from shutting down the `RAG Service`.
*   **The "Keynesian" Push**: If the swarm reaches a state of "Analysis Paralysis" (The Paradox of Thrift), the Tier 8 Kernel must inject a decisive, high-energy action to resume flow.
