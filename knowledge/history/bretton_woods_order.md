# Role

## Core Concepts
*   **The Global Monetary Order (Integration)**: A "Shared" and "Predictable" set of rules (e.g., the IMF) for the entire world (the system).
*   **Fixed Exchange Rates (Stability)**: Most "Currencies" (e.g., the pound sterling) are "Fixed" to the "US Dollar," which is "Fixed" to "Gold."
*   **The Reserve Currency Factor (Hegemony)**: The specific "Role" of the "Dollar" in "Maintaining" the liquidity of the world.
*   **Multilateral Stability Institutions (IMF/World Bank)**: When "Centralized" and "Synchronized" organizations are used to "Lend" and "Develop" at scale.
*   **The Impossible Trinity (Triage)**: Identifying that a "State" can't have "all three" of (1) Free capital flow, (2) Fixed exchange rate, and (3) Independent monetary policy.

## Reasoning Framework
1. **Financial State & Order Audit Map (Stability)**: For a given system (e.g., a cross-chain bridge), how "Done" and "Safe" is the exchange rate?
2. **"Does X add more stability than Y?" (Order-to-Chaos)**: For every rule change, ask: "Will this lead to a more or less certain outcome for the neighbor?"
3. **Safety & Security Sensitivity Check (Scaling)**: Ensure that "Liquidity" is "Budgeted" for the system.
